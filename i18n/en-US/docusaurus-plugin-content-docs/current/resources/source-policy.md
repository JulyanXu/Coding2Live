# Source and Copyright Policy

## Source Priority

1. Official documentation, standards, government, or international organization materials.
2. Papers, university courses, and public research reports.
3. Actively maintained open-source projects and reputable tool docs.
4. Reproducible tutorials, cases, and engineering practice notes.

## Avoid or Treat Carefully

- Reposts with unclear origin.
- Marketing pieces that provide conclusions without process.
- Paid materials whose core value cannot be cited without permission.
- Non-authoritative advice in medical, legal, financial, or other high-risk domains.

## Copyright Boundary

Coding2Live does not republish full articles or long copyrighted passages. Entries should summarize, evaluate, explain practice paths, and link back to original sources.

## Evaluation Criteria

- Credibility: is it official or maintained by a reputable source?
- Reproducibility: can a reader follow it?
- Freshness: could the tool, API, or policy expire?
- Risk clarity: are the boundaries stated?
